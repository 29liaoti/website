The Solution

Four genetic technologies
that changed everything

Modern genomics gave us an entirely new toolbox. Each technology targets a different part of the outbreak response cycle ‚Äî from detection, to diagnosis, to treatment and prevention.

üß¨ Identification

DNA / RNA Sequencing

Whole-genome sequencing allows scientists to read the complete genetic code of a pathogen within hours of isolating it. This powered global SARS-CoV-2 variant surveillance through databases like GISAID.

  • Identifies new variants and mutations in near real-time
  • Traces transmission chains and outbreak origins
  • Shares genomic data globally through open databases
  • Enables rapid vaccine target identification
üî¨ Diagnosis

PCR Testing

Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR) amplifies tiny amounts of viral genetic material to detectable levels ‚Äî providing the gold standard for highly sensitive, specific diagnostic testing.

  • Detects viral RNA/DNA from a nasal swab sample
  • Returns results within 2‚Äì6 hours at scale
  • Distinguishes viral strains with sequence specificity
  • Forms backbone of population-level surveillance
üíâ Prevention

mRNA Vaccines

Instead of introducing a weakened virus, mRNA vaccines deliver genetic instructions so your own cells produce a viral protein ‚Äî triggering immunity without infection. COVID-19 vaccines went from sequence to approved in under 12 months.

  • Designed computationally once the genome is sequenced
  • Faster to manufacture than traditional vaccines
  • Easily updated when variants emerge
  • Platform applicable to flu, HIV, cancer immunotherapy
‚úÇÔ∏è Treatment & Detection

CRISPR-Cas Technology

Originally a bacterial immune system, CRISPR has been repurposed both as an ultra-precise diagnostic tool (SHERLOCK, DETECTR) and as a therapeutic approach that can target and silence viral genes directly inside cells.

  • CRISPR diagnostics detect viral sequences with enzyme speed
  • Can identify COVID, flu, and HIV from a single sample
  • Therapeutic CRISPR targets viral DNA integrated into host cells
  • Under trial for HIV cure and hepatitis B treatment
Workflow

From outbreak to response:
The genetic pipeline

01

Outbreak Detected ‚Üí Sequence the Pathogen

When unusual illness clusters emerge, samples are sent to labs for whole-genome sequencing. Within 24‚Äì48 hours, scientists have the full genetic blueprint of the pathogen, enabling immediate comparison against known viruses and identification of mutation hotspots.

02

Design PCR Tests for Rapid Mass Diagnosis

Using the genomic sequence, diagnostic teams design PCR primers that target unique regions of the viral genome. These tests can be deployed to hospitals and testing centers within days, enabling population-level surveillance and contact tracing.

03

Develop mRNA Vaccine Targeting Viral Proteins

Scientists identify the viral surface protein (like the spike protein in SARS-CoV-2) and encode the mRNA instructions for it. Clinical trials begin within months; manufacturing can be scaled globally once the mRNA sequence is established, dramatically compressing the vaccine development timeline.

04

Deploy CRISPR for Next-Gen Diagnostics & Therapeutics

CRISPR-based diagnostics provide point-of-care testing without PCR machines. Meanwhile, therapeutic CRISPR programs target the genetic machinery of viruses that integrate into human cells, offering potential functional cures for conditions like HIV and chronic hepatitis B that current vaccines cannot address.
